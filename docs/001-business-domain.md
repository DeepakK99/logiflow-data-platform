# LogiFlow Analytics Platform

## Project Overview

This repository contains the design and implementation of a modern data platform for **LogiFlow**, a fictional Third-Party Logistics (3PL) company.

The goal of this project is to design, build, and evolve an enterprise-grade analytics platform capable of ingesting, processing, modeling, and serving logistics data for operational reporting, business intelligence, and executive decision-making.

This project is built as if it were a real client engagement, where our engineering team is responsible for designing and implementing a scalable data platform for LogiFlow.

---

# About LogiFlow

## Company Overview

LogiFlow is a global Third-Party Logistics (3PL) company that provides warehousing, transportation, shipment management, and delivery tracking services for businesses across multiple countries.

Rather than manufacturing products, LogiFlow helps businesses manage the storage and movement of their products throughout the supply chain.

Its customers include manufacturers, retailers, wholesalers, and e-commerce companies that rely on LogiFlow to manage their logistics operations efficiently.

---

# Business Services

LogiFlow offers the following core business services:

## Warehouse Management

* Stores products on behalf of customers.
* Manages warehouse inventory.
* Tracks stock movement between warehouses.
* Optimizes warehouse utilization.

## Shipment Management

* Creates and manages customer shipments.
* Plans shipment routes.
* Assigns carriers.
* Tracks shipment lifecycle.

## Transportation Management

* Coordinates transportation between warehouses and delivery locations.
* Optimizes routes for cost and delivery efficiency.
* Monitors carrier performance.

## Shipment Tracking

* Provides shipment status updates throughout the delivery lifecycle.
* Records operational events such as pickup, warehouse transfers, transit, and delivery.
* Enables customers to monitor shipment progress.

## Logistics Analytics

* Provides operational and executive insights.
* Tracks delivery performance.
* Measures warehouse utilization.
* Monitors service level agreement (SLA) compliance.
* Generates business KPIs for stakeholders.

# Business Scenario

LogiFlow has grown rapidly over the years and now operates:

* Multiple warehouses across several countries
* Multiple carrier partners
* Thousands of business customers
* Millions of shipments every month

As the company expanded, different operational teams adopted independent systems to manage their respective business functions.

Examples include:

* Warehouse Management System (WMS)
* Transportation Management System (TMS)
* Shipment Tracking Service
* Customer Management System
* Carrier Management System

Each system generates valuable operational data, but this data remains distributed across multiple sources.

---

# Problem Statement

Although LogiFlow's operational systems function effectively, the organization lacks a centralized analytics platform.

As a result:

* Data is scattered across multiple systems.
* Reporting is inconsistent between departments.
* Business users spend significant time preparing data before analysis.
* Executive dashboards are often delayed.
* Historical reporting is difficult.
* Data quality varies across different data sources.
* Operational teams lack a single source of truth.

Without a unified analytics platform, it becomes difficult for leadership to make timely, data-driven decisions.

---

# Project Objectives

The objective of this project is to design and build a centralized data platform that will:

* Collect data from multiple operational systems.
* Standardize and validate incoming data.
* Maintain a reliable historical record of business operations.
* Produce analytics-ready datasets.
* Enable business intelligence and executive reporting.
* Support future real-time analytics capabilities.

---

# Success Criteria

The platform should enable LogiFlow to answer questions such as:

* How many shipments were delivered on time?
* Which warehouses are operating at the highest utilization?
* Which carriers consistently miss delivery SLAs?
* Which customers generate the highest shipment volume?
* What are the most common shipment delays?
* How efficiently are products moving through the logistics network?

---

# Project Scope

This project focuses on building the analytics platform that powers reporting and decision-making.

The platform will consume data generated by existing operational systems but will **not** replace or implement those operational systems themselves.

---

# Future Vision

The platform is designed to evolve incrementally.

Future phases will introduce additional capabilities such as:

* Real-time event processing
* Streaming data pipelines
* Advanced monitoring
* Automated data quality checks
* Platform scalability improvements
* Production-grade operational workflows

This approach mirrors how modern enterprise data platforms evolve over time, beginning with reliable batch processing before expanding into real-time analytics and advanced platform capabilities.
