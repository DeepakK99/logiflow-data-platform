# Business Process

## Overview

This document describes the primary business process of LogiFlow and explains how shipments move through the organization from creation to successful delivery.

The purpose of this document is to establish a shared understanding of the business workflow before designing the data platform.

The analytics platform is built around this business process.

---

# Business Process

LogiFlow's primary business process is the fulfillment and transportation of customer shipments.

Business customers use LogiFlow to store their products in warehouses and request deliveries to customers, distributors, retailers, or other warehouses.

Every shipment follows a well-defined lifecycle consisting of multiple operational stages.

Each stage generates operational data that is later consumed by the analytics platform.

# Business Process Steps

## 1. Shipment Created

A business customer creates a shipment request using LogiFlow's logistics platform.

At this stage, a shipment record is immediately created with an initial status of **CREATED**.

The shipment includes information such as:

* Customer
* Origin warehouse
* Destination
* Products
* Quantities
* Requested delivery date
* Delivery priority

Creating the shipment at this stage allows the business to analyze all shipment requests, including those that are eventually rejected.

---

## 2. Shipment Validation

The Shipment Management System validates the shipment before execution.

Validation includes checks such as:

* Inventory availability
* Valid destination
* Service availability
* Business rules
* Delivery SLA feasibility

The shipment is either approved or rejected.

If rejected, the shipment remains in the system with a status of **REJECTED** along with a rejection reason.

Example rejection reasons include:

* Inventory unavailable
* Unsupported destination
* Invalid shipment details
* Customer account restrictions
* Hazardous goods restrictions

Rejected shipments are retained for operational analysis and reporting.

---

## 3. Inventory Reservation

For approved shipments, inventory is reserved inside the warehouse.

Reservation guarantees that products allocated to a shipment cannot be simultaneously assigned to another shipment.

---

## 4. Warehouse Fulfillment

Warehouse operators prepare the shipment for transportation.

Typical activities include:

* Picking products
* Packing products
* Labeling
* Pallet preparation

Once completed, the shipment becomes ready for pickup.

---

## 5. Carrier Assignment

The Transportation Management System assigns the shipment to a carrier.

Carrier selection depends on factors such as:

* Destination
* Cost
* Delivery SLA
* Carrier availability
* Route optimization

The assigned carrier becomes responsible for transporting the shipment.

---

## 6. Shipment Pickup

The carrier collects the shipment from the warehouse.

This marks the beginning of physical transportation.

The shipment status is updated accordingly.

---

## 7. Transportation

The shipment moves through the logistics network.

A shipment may travel through one or more logistics hubs before reaching its final destination.

Examples include:

* Regional distribution centers
* National distribution hubs
* International transit hubs

Each movement generates tracking events that become part of the shipment history.

Version 1 of the platform assumes a shipment originates from a single warehouse but may pass through multiple intermediate hubs before delivery.

---

## 8. Out For Delivery

The shipment reaches the destination delivery region and is assigned for final delivery.

The shipment status changes to **OUT_FOR_DELIVERY**.

---

## 9. Delivered

The shipment is successfully delivered to the destination.

Proof of delivery is recorded and the shipment status changes to **DELIVERED**.

---

## 10. Shipment Closed

After successful delivery, the shipment lifecycle is completed.

The shipment remains available for historical reporting and business analytics.

---

# Business Rules

The following business rules apply to the shipment lifecycle:

* Every shipment is created before validation.
* Every shipment has a unique Shipment ID.
* A shipment may contain one or more products.
* A shipment originates from a single warehouse.
* A shipment may pass through multiple logistics hubs during transportation.
* Every shipment maintains a current status.
* Every status change generates a tracking event.
* Rejected shipments remain in the system for historical analysis.
* Delivered shipments are never physically deleted from the platform.

# Analytics Opportunities

Each stage of the shipment lifecycle provides valuable business insights.

| Lifecycle Stage       | Example Business Questions                          |
| --------------------- | --------------------------------------------------- |
| Shipment Created      | How many shipments are requested each day?          |
| Shipment Validation   | What percentage of shipments are rejected?          |
| Inventory Reservation | How often do inventory shortages delay shipments?   |
| Warehouse Fulfillment | Which warehouses require the most processing time?  |
| Carrier Assignment    | Which carriers are selected most frequently?        |
| Shipment Pickup       | How quickly are shipments picked up after approval? |
| Transportation        | Which routes experience the most delays?            |
| Out For Delivery      | How long does final-mile delivery take?             |
| Delivered             | What is the on-time delivery percentage?            |
| Shipment Closed       | What are the long-term delivery performance trends? |

# Scope Decisions

The initial version of the platform includes the following assumptions:

## Included

* Multiple products per shipment
* Single origin warehouse per shipment
* Multiple transportation hubs
* Shipment approval and rejection tracking
* Complete shipment lifecycle history
* Historical analytics

## Out of Scope (Future Enhancements)

* Multi-origin shipments
* Shipment splitting
* Shipment merging
* Partial deliveries
* Reverse logistics and returns
* Cross-docking optimization

These capabilities may be introduced in future iterations of the platform as the business evolves.

# Relationship to the Analytics Platform

This business process defines the operational workflow of LogiFlow.

The data generated at each stage of the shipment lifecycle will be ingested into the analytics platform, where it will be standardized, transformed, modeled, and exposed for reporting and business intelligence.

This document serves as the business foundation upon which the remainder of the platform architecture will be designed.
